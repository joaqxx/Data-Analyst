Business Scenario

The coffee chain's executive team required a consolidated, real-time view of sales performance. Data was isolated across separate tables (Orders, Customers, Products), preventing unified analysis of revenue trends, customer value, and product success.

Project Scope

  • Data Quality: Assessing and cleaning a raw dataset of 1,000 customer records.
  • Data Transformation: Engineering new features (Age Brackets) to enable categorical analysis.
  • Visualization: Designing a dynamic, single-page dashboard with 7 interactive charts.
  • Strategy: Deriving concrete marketing recommendations based on conversion rate analysis.

Methodology & Technical Implementation

Data Cleaning

  • De-duplication: Identified and removed 26 duplicate records to ensure accurate customer counting.
  • Standardization: "Find & Replace" functionality was used to normalize inconsistent data entries (e.g., standardizing "M/F" to "Male/Female" and "S/M" to "Single/Married") for cleaner visualization labels.
  • Currency Formatting: Income columns were adjusted to a zero-decimal currency format for improved readability across all reporting sheets.

Data Modeling

  • Age Bracketing: A nested IF statement formula was developed to categorize numeric ages into strategic groups ("Adolescent," "Middle Age," "Old"). This modeling step was essential for enabling trend analysis across generational cohorts rather than individual ages.
  • Pivot Table Architecture: A total of 7 distinct Pivot Tables were built to drive the interactive dashboard. Crucially, for the Occupation and Region analysis, the "Show Values As % of Row Total" feature was utilized. This was a strategic decision to calculate Conversion Rate (efficiency) rather than just raw volume, providing a more accurate metric for marketing ROI.

Key Insights & Recommendations
Finding #1
Overall Performance
The dataset reveals an overall conversion rate of roughly 48%, indicating a relatively balanced split between buyers and non-buyers, but with specific segments performing significantly better.
Finding #2
Occupation Impact
"Professionals" have the highest likelihood of purchasing a bike (~54% conversion rate), whereas "Manual" and "Clerical" roles purchase at rates below 50%.
Finding #3
Utility & Transportation Drivers
Customers exhibit a high propensity to buy when a bike serves a utility purpose. This is evidenced by a strong negative correlation between car ownership and bike purchase, as well as the fact that the most frequent buyers commute 0‚Äì1 Miles. This suggests the bikes are being used for short-distance transport rather than primarily for recreation.
Recommendations
  • Shift a larger percentage of the digital ad budget to the Pacific Region to capitalize on the higher conversion efficiency found there.
  • Exclude households with 2+ cars from primary targeting to reduce wasted ad spend.
  • Tailor marketing imagery to feature Middle-Aged Professionals using bikes for short commutes, mirroring the lifestyle of the highest-converting user base.
